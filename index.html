Prince George’s County COVID-19 Childcare Quarantine and Isolation Guidelines

If You Test Positive for COVID-19 (Isolate)

All persons who test positive for  COVID-19 or have suspected COVID 19, regardless of vaccination status

STAFF: 

• Stay home for at least 5 full days from the symptom onset date if symptomatic, or the date of the positive  test if no symptoms. 

• On Day 6, if there are no symptoms or symptoms are  resolved and no fever for at least 24 hours without  medication, staff may return to work. 

ATTENDEES: 

• Stay home for at least 5 full days from the symptom onset date if symptomatic, or the date of the positive  test if no symptoms. 

• On Day 6, if there are no symptoms or symptoms are  resolved and no fever for at least 24 hours without  medication, attendee may return to childcare. 

Day 0 = Symptom Onset Date or Date Test was  Performed (Specimen collection date, NOT result  date)

Prince George’s County COVID-19 Childcare Quarantine and Isolation Guidelines

NOTES: 

A 10-day isolation must be used for anyone unable to  maintain the following mitigations strategies: 

• Consistent and correct use of a well-fitting mask  at all times, except eating. 

• Social distancing (6 feet or more between all staff  and attendees, including when masks are 

removed for meals, snacks, and napping). 

• Frequent and effective hand hygiene. 

• Cohorting staff and students throughout the day  • Avoid sharing of items between individuals,  classrooms, and cohorts 

• Adequate ventilation 

• Frequent cleaning of high touch areas and items

If You Were Exposed to Someone with COVID-19

If you are asymptomatic and: 

Have been boosted  

OR 

Completed the primary series of Pfizer vaccine within the last 5 months  OR 

Completed the primary series of  Moderna vaccine within the last 6  months  

OR 

Completed the primary series of J&J  vaccine within the last 2 months OR 

Had confirmed COVID-19 within the  last 90 days

• You Do Not need to quarantine after exposure. • Wear a well-fitting mask around others for 10 days  after the last date of exposure with the positive  person for COVID-19 

• Must test on Day 5 after the date of last exposure.  (Persons who had a confirmed positive COVID-19 test  within the last 90 days do not need to test). 

o Rapid Antigen or PCR test is acceptable.  

Day 0 = Date of Last Exposure 

o If the person tests positive or develops  

symptoms, they should stay home and follow  

recommendations for “If You Test Positive for  

COVID-19 (Isolate)”. 

o If the person tests negative, they should  

continue to wear a well-fitting mask around  

others for 10 days from the last date of  

exposure. 

Note:  

• Persons who do not meet the vaccination criteria but  had COVID-19 within the past 90 days can continue  to work or attend childcare if they remain  

asymptomatic. If symptoms occur, the person should  be immediately excluded and recommended for  testing. 

• If the person is unable to wear a well-fitting mask  correctly and consistently at all times, they should  stay home for 10 full days from the date of last  exposure

Prince George’s County COVID-19 Childcare Quarantine and Isolation Guidelines

OUTBREAK DEFINITION: 

Family Child Care Homes: 

Two or more laboratory-confirmed COVID-19 cases  among 

providers, provider’s household members, or attendees  with 

onsets (or, if asymptomatic, collection dates) within a  14-day 

period, and who are epidemiologically linked. Child Care Center: 

Two or more laboratory-confirmed COVID-19 cases  among staff 

or attendees with onsets (or, if asymptomatic,  collection dates) 

within a 14-day period, and who are epidemiologically  linked, 

but not household contacts. 

Note: To open an outbreak investigation, cases must  be from at least two separates’ households.

If you have an outbreak situation do the following: 

• Immediately exclude all positive cases  

• Identify and quarantine all close contacts  

• Temporarily close the affected classroom(s) (if a  center) or close the home childcare for assessment  and deep cleaning  

• Contact the Health Department for further guidance  and timeline for reopening. 

Note: Outbreaks will be evaluated and managed on a  case-by-case basis depending on each specific situation.  Recommendations or additional guidance may be  provided beyond the updated Isolation and Quarantine  Guideline, to mitigate transmission during the outbreak.

Special Notes: 

Persons who are immunocompromised or are unable to maintain mitigation strategies including wearing a well-fitting mask consistently and correctly at  all times except when eating or napping, are not eligible for the 5-day isolation or quarantine and must complete a 10 day isolation or quarantine for 10  days from the last date of exposure.  

o Testing prior to return from a 10-day quarantine is recommended but not required

Persons who are a household contacts of a confirmed case with COVID-19 and they are unable to avoid further contact with their ill household member  may return to the facility once a minimum 5-day quarantine (depending on use of mitigation strategies, including consistent and correct mask use) period is  completed after the positive person in the household is released from isolation.  

o If the positive persons isolation ends after Day 5, the household contact quarantine would begin on day 6.  

o If the positive persons isolation ends after day 10, the household contact quarantine would begin on day 11.

The person must undergo this additional quarantine period because the person could have been infected on the final day of the household member’s  isolation. 

If a person develops symptoms, after testing on day 5, they should immediately be excluded if they have returned to the facility, or remain home, if  they have not returned to the facility and get tested.  

o If the test is positive, they should follow the recommendations for “If You Test Positive for COVID-19 (Isolate)”.

o If the test is negative, they may return when applicable criteria in the Communicable Diseases Summary have been met. To report positive or exposed cases or an outbreak situation, please call 301-583-3751 or 301-263-3878
